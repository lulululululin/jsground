The Limits of Art 艺术的极限

On Borderline 在边界上

Cases of Artworks and their Aesthetic Properties 艺术品案例及其美学特性
对于“艺术的边界”，你觉得用英文应该怎么说
"艺术的边界" 在英文中可以表达为 "the boundaries of art" 或 "the limits of art"。这个短语用来探讨艺术的定义、艺术作品的接受范围以及艺术与非艺术之间的区分。
那如果说一位作家写的是borderline cases of art，这是什么意思呢
如果说一位作家写的是 "borderline cases of art"，这通常意味着他们的作品处于艺术定义的边缘，可能挑战或模糊了传统艺术的界限。这些作品可能在风格、内容、形式或表达方式上与传统艺术有所不同，使得它们难以被明确归类。这样的作品往往引发关于艺术本质和艺术界线的重要讨论。简而言之，这位作家的作品可能被认为是艺术与非艺术之间的模糊地带，或者是对艺术常规的挑战和探索。
那他为什么不用 boundaries 呢？
使用 "borderline" 而不是 "boundaries" 在这个语境中，作家可能是为了强调作品的模糊性和争议性。"Borderline" 这个词通常用来描述那些处于某个分类或状态边缘的事物，暗示着它们可能不完全符合既定的标准或分类。在艺术领域，这可能意味着作品在艺术性的认定上存在争议，或者它们可能在挑战现有的艺术标准和观众的预期。
相比之下，"boundaries" 这个词更倾向于描述明确的界限或范围。当人们谈论 "the boundaries of art" 时，他们通常在讨论艺术的一般范围和限制。而 "borderline cases" 则更具体地指向那些处于这些界限边缘的特殊实例，这些实例可能引发关于艺术定义的深入讨论。
因此，选择 "borderline cases" 可能是为了强调作品的独特性和它们在艺术界边缘的地位，以及它们可能引起的讨论和思考。

Chapter 1‌‌ [章]

Introduction: Different Types of Limits

简介：不同类型的限制

§1. This book is about exploring interesting borderline cases of art. I’ll discuss the cases of gustatory and olfactory artworks (focusing on food), proprioceptive artworks (dance, martial arts, and rock climbing qua proprioceptive experiences), intellectual artworks (philosophical and scientific theories), as well as the vague limits between painting and photography. Perhaps you’ll find it obvious that some or all of these cases are genuine cases of art, and that the claims I will be making are trivial. In that case, I’ll be happy to agree and I hope that this book will still be of interest as a fruitful discussion of these cases of artworks and their limits. Perhaps you’ll find it obvious that some or all of these cases are not genuine cases of art—indeed, all of them, individually, have been denied the status of artworks at some point. In that case, I’ll be happy to disagree and I’ll try to offer reasons to change your mind. My aim in this short book is thus twofold. First, I hope that the discussion of this series of particular claims about these particular cases will be of interest for its own, first-level, sake; and second, from this discussion, a more general picture about the nature of art and about what counts as an artwork will arise. Indeed, the different cases will allow us to consider different types of limits. Some limits will concern our senses (our different perceptual modalities), some will concern vagueness and fuzzy boundaries between different types of works of art, some will concern the amount of human intention and intervention in the process of creation of an artwork, and some will concern the border between art and science. In these various ways, by understanding better such borderline cases, we will—or so I hope—get a better grip on an understanding of the nature of art.
第 1 条。这本书是关于探索有趣的艺术边缘案例。我将讨论味觉艺术和嗅觉艺术作品（专注于食物）、本体感受艺术作品（舞蹈、武术和攀岩准本体感受体验）、智力艺术作品（哲学和科学理论）以及绘画和摄影之间的模糊界限。也许你会发现，很明显，这些案例差不多吧，都是真实的艺术案例，而我将要提出的主张是微不足道的。在这种情况下，我很乐意同意，我希望这本书仍然会引起人们的兴趣，因为它是对这些艺术品案例及其局限性的富有成效的讨论。也许你会发现，很明显，这些 案例中的一些或全部都不是真正的艺术案例——事实上，所有这些案例，单独地，在某个时候都被剥夺了艺术品的地位。在这种情况下，我很乐意不同意，我会尽量提供改变主意的理由。因此，我在这本短书中有两个目的。首先，我希望关于这些特定案件的这一系列特定主张的讨论本身就会引起人们的兴趣，因为这是第一层次 的;其次，从这次讨论中，将出现关于艺术本质以及什么是艺术品的更一般的图景。事实上，不同的情况将使我们能够考虑不同类型的限制。有些限制涉及我们的感 官（我们不同的感知方式），有些涉及不同类型艺术作品之间的模糊和模糊界限，有些涉及人类在创作过程中的意图和干预的数量，有些涉及艺术与科学之间的边 界。 通过这些不同的方式，通过更好地理解这些边缘案例，我们将——或者我希望——更好地理解艺术的本质。

§2. “What is art?” is a very general and cruelly difficult question. Perhaps, it is so cruelly difficult precisely because it is so very general. Indeed, any precise answer, that is, any definition of art, or artwork, which would precisely define what counts as art and what does not, is always in principle at great risk of leaving some possibilities out and of being open to possible counter-examples. The notion of art, or artwork, is a vague one but this is not only a problem of vagueness. The difficulty of providing a definition also stems from the fact that art evolves with time, that it is culture-relative, and that we need to adapt our understanding of what art is to this

ever-evolving process. A strict definition of what counts as art and what does not is bound to fail at some point. But not having a definition of something does not mean that this something is inexplicable (contra Adajian (2018, §1)). Indeed, if we keep in mind the ever-evolving nature of art, we can simply realize that there is no need for a definition (why would it be necessary to have one?) and that we simply should continuously update our understanding of what art is, and allow ourselves to be surprised from time to time when new, challenging forms of art emerge. Any definition that would try, for instance, to identify what is common to all artworks would simply be inadequate, perhaps precisely in the sense—highly relevant to the overall purposes of this book—that it would beg the question against non-standard types of artworks and classify them as not being art. In order to resist such a possibly question-begging stance, we can simply resist the need for a definition.1 Walton (2007, p. 148) even suggested that the question does not even make much sense: “It is not at all clear that these words—‘What is art?’—express anything like a single question, to which competing answers are given […] The sheer variety of proposed definitions should give us pause. One cannot help wondering whether there is any sense in which they are attempts to […] address the same issue”. This does not mean, however, that we should somehow become sceptical about the very notion of art and that we should abandon the very idea of trying to understand what an artwork is. On the contrary, the lack of a precise and fixed definition is all the more reason to try to improve our understanding of the nature of art, while embracing its impermanence and vagueness. 不 断发展的过程。对什么算作艺术，什么不算艺术的严格定义，在某些时候注定会失败。但是，没有对某物的定义并不意味着该物是无法解释的（contra Adajian （2018 ， §1））。事实上，如果我们牢记艺术不断发展的本质，我们就可以简单地意识到，没有必要有一个定义（为什么有必要有一个定义？），我们只是应该不断更新我 们对艺术的理解，并允许自己不时地感到惊讶，当新的， 具有挑战性的艺术形式应运而生。例如，任何试图确定所有艺术品的共同点的定义都是不充分的，也许恰恰是在某种意义上——与本书的总体目的高度相关——它会 回避非标准类型的艺术品并将它们归类为非艺术。1 沃尔顿（2007 年，第 148 页）甚至认为这个问题甚至没有多大意义：“根本不清楚这些词——'什么是艺术？- 表达任何类似单个问题的内容，给出相互竞争的答案 [...]拟议的定义种类繁多，应该让我们停下来。人们不禁要问，他们试图解决同样的问题是否有任何意义”。然而，这并不意味着我们应该以某种方式对艺术 的概念本身持怀疑态度，我们应该放弃试图理解艺术品是什么的想法。相反，缺乏精确和固定的定义，更有理由试图提高我们对艺术本质的理解，同时拥抱它的无常 和模糊。

§3. To this end, let me list some points I propose to take on board as working criteria for a discussion about what counts as an artwork and what does not. Keeping in mind what I said above, these do not constitute a set of necessary and sufficient conditions. Rather, they are merely indicators or pointers that help us clarify the discussion about the nature of art. I take it that this list is rather standard (although it is, of course, controversial),2 and I am simply going to use these points as more or less optional guidelines, by examining whether they apply to the various cases I am going to discuss below and whether they are useful or not. As a general rule, I’ll work with the idea that if something does not satisfy all of these points, this is not automatically a reason to think that it is not an artwork. §3 . 为此，让我列出一些我建议采纳的要点，作为讨论什么算作艺术品，什么不算艺术品的工作标准。牢记我上面所说的，这些并不构成一套必要和充分的条件。相反， 它们只是帮助我们澄清关于艺术本质的讨论的指标或指针。我认为这个列表是相当标准的（尽管它当然是有争议的），2 我只是将这些要点用作或多或少的可选指南，通过检查它们是否适用于我将在下面讨论的各种情况以及它们是否有用。作为一般规则，我会这样的想法是，如果某样 东西不满足所有这些要点，这不会自动成为认为它不是艺术品的理由。
