The Limits of Art 艺术的极限

On Borderline 在边界上

Cases of Artworks and their Aesthetic Properties 艺术品案例及其美学特性
对于"艺术的边界"，你觉得用英文应该怎么说
"艺术的边界" 在英文中可以表达为 "the boundaries of art" 或 "the limits of art"。这个短语用来探讨艺术的定义、艺术作品的接受范围以及艺术与非艺术之间的区分。
那如果说一位作家写的是borderline cases of art，这是什么意思呢
如果说一位作家写的是 "borderline cases of art"，这通常意味着他们的作品处于艺术定义的边缘，可能挑战或模糊了传统艺术的界限。这些作品可能在风格、内容、形式或表达方式上与传统艺术有所不同，使得它们难以被明确归类。这样的作品往往引发关于艺术本质和艺术界线的重要讨论。简而言之，这位作家的作品可能被认为是艺术与非艺术之间的模糊地带，或者是对艺术常规的挑战和探索。
那他为什么不用 boundaries 呢？
使用 "borderline" 而不是 "boundaries" 在这个语境中，作家可能是为了强调作品的模糊性和争议性。"Borderline" 这个词通常用来描述那些处于某个分类或状态边缘的事物，暗示着它们可能不完全符合既定的标准或分类。在艺术领域，这可能意味着作品在艺术性的认定上存在争议，或者它们可能在挑战现有的艺术标准和观众的预期。
相比之下，"boundaries" 这个词更倾向于描述明确的界限或范围。当人们谈论 "the boundaries of art" 时，他们通常在讨论艺术的一般范围和限制。而 "borderline cases" 则更具体地指向那些处于这些界限边缘的特殊实例，这些实例可能引发关于艺术定义的深入讨论。
因此，选择 "borderline cases" 可能是为了强调作品的独特性和它们在艺术界边缘的地位，以及它们可能引起的讨论和思考。

Chapter 1. Introduction: Different Types of Limits

第一章 简介：不同类型的限制

§1. This book is about exploring interesting borderline cases of art. I'll discuss the cases of gustatory and olfactory artworks (focusing on food), proprioceptive artworks (dance, martial arts, and rock climbing qua proprioceptive experiences), intellectual artworks (philosophical and scientific theories), as well as the vague limits between painting and photography. Perhaps you'll find it obvious that some or all of these cases are genuine cases of art, and that the claims I will be making are trivial. In that case, I'll be happy to agree and I hope that this book will still be of interest as a fruitful discussion of these cases of artworks and their limits. Perhaps you'll find it obvious that some or all of these cases are not genuine cases of art—indeed, all of them, individually, have been denied the status of artworks at some point. In that case, I'll be happy to disagree and I'll try to offer reasons to change your mind. My aim in this short book is thus twofold. First, I hope that the discussion of this series of particular claims about these particular cases will be of interest for its own, first-level, sake; and second, from this discussion, a more general picture about the nature of art and about what counts as an artwork will arise. Indeed, the different cases will allow us to consider different types of limits. Some limits will concern our senses (our different perceptual modalities), some will concern vagueness and fuzzy boundaries between different types of works of art, some will concern the amount of human intention and intervention in the process of creation of an artwork, and some will concern the border between art and science. In these various ways, by understanding better such borderline cases, we will—or so I hope—get a better grip on an understanding of the nature of art.
第 1 节。这本书是关于探索有趣的艺术边缘案例。我将讨论味觉艺术和嗅觉艺术作品（专注于食物）、本体感受艺术作品（舞蹈、武术和攀岩准本体感受体验）、智力艺术作品（哲学和科学理论）以及绘画和摄影之间的模糊界限。也许你会觉得这些案例很明显都是真实的艺术案例！那我很乐意同意这一点，（在这种情况下）我将要提出的（它们作为艺术品的权利的索赔）主张是微不足道的，但我仍然希望这本书作为对这些艺术品及其局限性的讨论可以是有趣并富有成效的，仍然会引起人们的兴趣。但你也可能会认为，很明显这些案例中的一些甚或全部并不能称为真正的艺术作品，而且的确，这些案例都各自在某个时候被剥夺了艺术品的地位。如果是这样的话，那我很乐意提出不同的看法，并尽量提供反对的理由。 因此，我在这本小书中有两个目的。 首先，我希望关于这些作品是否有资格称为艺术品的讨论本身会引起人们的兴趣，这是最第一层, sake； 第二，从这次讨论中，将出现关于艺术本质以及什么是艺术品的更一般的图景。 事实上，不同的情况将使我们能够考虑不同类型的限制。有些限制涉及我们的感官（我们不同的感知方式），有些涉及不同类型艺术作品之间的模糊和模糊界限，有些涉及人类在创作过程中的意图和干预的量，有些涉及艺术与科学之间的边界。通过这些不同的方式，通过更好地理解这些边缘案例，我们将————或者我希望————更好地理解艺术的本质。

§2. "What is art?" is a very general and cruelly difficult question. Perhaps, it is so cruelly difficult precisely because it is so very general. Indeed, any precise answer, that is, any definition of art, or artwork, which would precisely define what counts as art and what does not, is always in principle at great risk of leaving some possibilities out and of being open to possible counter-examples. The notion of art, or artwork, is a vague one but this is not only a problem of vagueness. The difficulty of providing a definition also stems from the fact that art evolves with time, that it is culture-relative, and that we need to adapt our understanding of what art is to this ever-evolving process. A strict definition of what counts as art and what does not is bound to fail at some point. But not having a definition of something does not mean that this something is inexplicable (Contra Adajian ( 2018 , §1)). Indeed, if we keep in mind the ever-evolving nature of art, we can simply realize that there is no need for a definition (why would it be necessary to have one?) and that we simply should continuously update our understanding of what art is, and allow ourselves to be surprised from time to time when new, challenging forms of art emerge. Any definition that would try, for instance, to identify what is common to all artworks would simply be inadequate, perhaps precisely in the sense—highly relevant to the overall purposes of this book—that it would beg the question against non-standard types of artworks and classify them as not being art. In order to resist such a possibly question-begging stance, we can simply resist the need for a definition.[1] Walton (2007, p. 148) even suggested that the question does not even make much sense: "It is not at all clear that these words —— 'What is art?' —— express anything like a single question, to which competing answers are given […] The sheer variety of proposed definitions should give us pause. One cannot help wondering whether there is any sense in which they are attempts to […] address the same issue". This does not mean, however, that we should somehow become sceptical about the very notion of art and that we should abandon the very idea of trying to understand what an artwork is. On the contrary, the lack of a precise and fixed definition is all the more reason to try to improve our understanding of the nature of art, while embracing its impermanence and vagueness.
第 2 节。"什么是艺术？"是一个极笼统极困难的问题。之所以如此严酷的难以精确定义它，也许恰恰是因为它是如此的“一般”。事实上，任何精确的答案，即任何对艺术或艺术品的定义，都能准确地定义什么是艺术，什么不算艺术，原则上总是有很大的风险，可能会遗漏一些真正的艺术品，却对可能的假艺术持开放态度（定义定的不够懂）。艺术或艺术品的概念是一个模糊的概念，但这不仅仅是一个模糊的问题。给艺术下定义的困难还源于这样一个事实，即艺术是随着时间而发展的，它是与文化相关的，我们需要调整我们对艺术的理解，以适应这一不断发展的过程。对什么算作艺术，什么不算艺术的严格定义，在某些时候注定会失败。但是，没有对某物的定义并不意味着该物是无法解释的（Contra Adajian （2018 ， §1））。事实上，如果我们牢记艺术不断发展的本质，我们就可以简单地意识到，没有必要有一个定义（为什么有必要有一个定义？），我们只是应该不断更新我们对艺术的理解，并允许自己不时地感到惊讶，当新的，具有挑战性的艺术形式应运而生。例如，任何试图确定所有艺术品的共同点的定义都是不充分的，也许恰恰是在某种意义上————与本书的总体目的高度相关————它会回避非标准类型的艺术品并将它们归类为非艺术。沃尔顿（2007 年，第 148 页）甚至认为这个问题甚至没有多大意义："根本不清楚这些词————'什么是艺术？- 表达任何类似单个问题的内容，给出相互竞争的答案 ... ... 拟议的定义种类繁多，应该让我们停下来。人们不禁要问，他们试图解决同样的问题是否有任何意义"。然而，这并不意味着我们应该以某种方式对艺术的概念本身持怀疑态度，我们应该放弃试图理解艺术品是什么的想法。相反，缺乏精确和固定的定义，更有理由试图提高我们对艺术本质的理解，同时拥抱它的无常和模糊。

§3. To this end, let me list some points I propose to take on board as working criteria for a discussion about what counts as an artwork and what does not. Keeping in mind what I said above, these do not constitute a set of necessary and sufficient conditions. Rather, they are merely indicators or pointers that help us clarify the discussion about the nature of art. I take it that this list is rather standard (although it is, of course, controversial),[2] and I am simply going to use these points as more or less optional guidelines, by examining whether they apply to the various cases I am going to discuss below and whether they are useful or not. As a general rule, I’ll work with the idea that if something does not satisfy all of these points, this is not automatically a reason to think that it is not an artwork. §3 .
为此，让我列出一些我建议采纳的要点，作为讨论什么算作艺术品，什么不算艺术品的工作标准。牢记我上面所说的，这些并不构成一套必要和充分的条件。相反， 它们只是帮助我们澄清关于艺术本质的讨论的指标或指针。我认为这个列表是相当标准的（尽管它当然是有争议的），我只是将这些要点用作或多或少的选择性指南，通过检查它们是否适用于我将在下面讨论的各种情况以及它们是否有用。我将这些列表作为一套通用规则，但如果某样东西不满足所有这些要点，我并不会直接就认定它就不是艺术品了。

  1. Artworks possess aesthetic properties 艺术品具有审美特性
  2. Artworks are subject to aesthetic judgements 艺术品受审美判断的影响
  3. Artworks have the capacity to trigger aesthetic experiences 艺术作品有能力引发审美体验
  4. Artworks have the capacity to trigger emotions 艺术品具有触发情感的能力
  5. Artworks have the capacity to convey meanings and ideas 艺术品具有传达意义和想法的能力
  6. Artworks are challenging (both for the artist and the observer) 艺术作品是具有挑战性的（对艺术家和观察者来说都是如此）
  7. Artworks require skill to be produced 艺术品需要技巧才能制作
  8. Artworks qua objects can have relevant non-aesthetic features and a non-aesthetic function. 艺术品可以具有相关的非审美特征和非审美功能。

(1), (2), (3), and (4) lie at the heart of what I want to take on board as a rough idea of what art is. (Perhaps, (3) and (4) are about the same thing.) In the following chapters, I am going to put these criteria to work, and see what can be learned by applying them to quite non-standard cases.
（1）、（2）、（3）和（4）是我想作为对艺术是什么的粗略概念的核心。（也许，（3）和（4）是一回事。在接下来的章节中，我将把这些标准付诸实践，看看通过将它们应用于非常不标准的案例可以学到什么。

§4. In addition to these criteria, there are some notions that emerge as being characterizations of works of art that we often appeal to when we talk about art. Let me quickly mention them, since they will also prove to be useful in the discussion of the non-standard cases I am going to focus on in the chapters to come. Harmony (and related notions such as symmetry) is perhaps the most often cited. In Western culture at least, the idea of something that is a coherently integrated whole, where proportion, symmetry, and harmony play an important role, is often central to the way we evaluate it as being a (good) piece of art. While Aristotle insisted especially on symmetry, Plato insisted on unity, and the way we evaluate artworks today has inherited and developed both of these criteria, at least when it comes to classical and standard works of art. The notion of balance is also often put forward, relatedly to harmony, unity, symmetry, and similar notions. These notions are of course implemented in different ways in different kinds of artworks such as music, sculpture, painting, or architecture, but they seem to provide a relatively common ground to such different types of cases.
第4节。除了这些标准之外，还有一些概念是我们在谈论艺术时经常诉诸的艺术作品的特征。让我快速地提到它们，因为它们也将证明在讨论“非标准案例”时是有用的， 我将在后面的章节中重点讨论。和谐（以及相关概念，如对称性）可能是最常被引用的。至少在西方文化中，一个连贯的整体，比例、对称和和谐起着重要作用，此概念往往是我们评估一件艺术品优劣的核心。虽然亚里士多德特别坚持对称性，但柏拉图坚持统一性，我们今天评估艺术品的方式继承并发展了这两个标准，至少在古典和标准艺术作品方面是这样。平衡的概念也经常被提出，与和谐、统一、对称和类似的概念有关。当然，这些概念在音乐、雕塑、绘画或建筑等不同类型的艺术品中以不同的方式实现，但它们似乎为这些不同类型的案例提供了一个相对共同的基础。

The notions of complexity and simplicity also often play an important role. While being contradictory, they can both be seen as positive features of an artwork and characterize its aesthetic value. A complex work can be intriguing, intricate, and sophisticated; it can be baroquely appealing, and its overall balance will often be emphasized. Simplicity will often be taken to be elegant in a straightforward way— indeed, simplicity and elegance just seem to go together.
复杂性和简单性的概念也经常起着重要作用。虽然是矛盾的，但它们既可以被视为艺术品的积极特征，也可以表征其美学价值。复杂的(complex)工作可以是有趣的、错乱的或微妙的; 它可以具有巴洛克式的吸引力，并且通常会强调其整体平衡。简单往往会以一种直截了当的方式被认为是优雅的——事实上，简单和优雅似乎是相辅相成的。

Intensity is another feature of works of art that often plays a crucial role in their appreciation and evaluation. It can manifest itself in different forms, but perhaps what it relates to on a general level is the traditional idea insisted upon by Burke that beauty causes passion in the observer. This lies at the heart of criteria (3) and (4) above, offering a response-dependent understanding of what makes something to be art. As Hume (1740, 299) puts it, beauty “[…] is fitted to give a pleasure and satisfaction to the soul. Pleasure and pain, therefore, are not only necessary attendants of beauty and deformity, but constitute their very essence.” He adds that “beauty is no quality in things themselves: it exists merely in the mind which contemplates them; and each mind perceives a different beauty” (Hume 1757, 136). This kind of subjectivism is useful to be taken on board; it will, however, need to be weakened and refined (as Hume himself did) in order to account for the fact that more or less objective judgements of the aesthetic value of an artwork can be made—this will be apparent in my discussion of the different types of cases, and I’ll come back to this general issue in due course.
剧烈是艺术作品的另一个特征，通常在欣赏和评估中起着至关重要的作用。它可以以不同的形式表现出来，但也许它在一般层面上与伯克坚持的传统观念有关，即美会引起观察者的激情。这是上文标准（3）和（4）的核心，提供了对是什么使某物成为艺术的反应依赖性理解。正如休谟（1740,299）所说，美“[...]适合给灵魂带来愉悦和满足。因此，快乐和痛苦不 仅是美和畸形的必要伴随物，而且构成了它们的本质。他补充说，“美不是事物本身的品质：它只存在于思考它们的头脑中;每个人的心灵都感知到不同的美“（休 谟 1757， 136）。这种主观主义是有用的;然而，它需要被削弱和完善（就像休谟本人所做的那样），以便解释这样一个事实，即可以对艺术品的审美价值做出或多或少的 客观判断——这在我对不同类型案例的讨论中是显而易见的，我将在适当的时候回到这个一般性问题。

In addition to the criteria (1)–(8), let us then keep in mind: harmony, symmetry, unity, balance, complexity, simplicity, intensity, passion, pleasure; and let us start exploring some non-standard cases of art.
除了标准（1）-（8）之外，让我们牢记：和谐、对称、统一、平衡、复杂、简单、强度、激情、愉悦;让我们开始探索一些非标准的艺术案例。

Chapter 2. Extending the Limits I: Non-visual and Non-auditory Artworks

第二章 扩展极限I：非视觉和非听觉艺术作品

Abstract 摘要

We talk about culinary arts, as we also talk about martial arts. Does this mean that a meal or a kata are to be understood as being artworks? In this chapter, I take these and other cases seriously and I claim that they are indeed genuine cases of art, in the same way as paintings, photographs, sculptures, or musical works. My centre of interest concerns non-visual and non-auditory forms of art. Traditionally, visual and auditory arts dominate the debate in aesthetics and philosophy of art, as they dominate the art world in general—our senses of sight and hearing thus dominate our very understanding of what an artwork is. As a consequence, artworks are mostly understood as being public entities, widely shareable in the sense of being available to different spectators/auditors. In this chapter, I explore other senses, which are often neglected; I focus on gastronomic meals as cases of gustatory and olfactory artworks, and on dance, karate, and climbing as cases of proprioceptive artworks. The overall aim of this chapter is thus to extend the limits of what counts as art and what does not. I offer a very inclusive view, and through the discussion of various types of examples, I offer reasons to think that such a view is indeed adequate.
我们谈论烹饪艺术，同时也谈论武术。这是否意味着一顿饭或一场武术应该被理解为艺术品？在本章中，我认真对待这些案例和其他案例，并声称它们确实是真实的艺术案例，就像绘画、照片、雕塑或音乐作品一样。我的兴趣中心涉及非视觉和非听觉的艺术形式。传统上，视觉和听觉艺术在美学和艺术哲学的辩论中占主导地位，因为它们在整个艺术界占主导地位——因此，我们的视觉和听觉主宰了我们对艺术品的理解。因此，艺术品大多被理解为公共实体，可以广泛共享，因为不同的观众/旁听者都可以使用。在本章中，我探讨了其他经常被忽视的感官；我专注于将美食作为味觉和嗅觉艺术品的案例，并将舞蹈、空手道和攀岩作为本体感受艺术品的案例。因此，本章的总体目标是扩展什么是艺术，什么不是艺术的界限。我提出了一个非常包容的观点，通过对各种例子的讨论，我提供了理由认为这种观点确实是充分的。

Introduction 介绍

§1. To start the discussion, let me indulge in a short biographical narrative and recount an experience I recently had in the restaurant of the famous French chef Marc Veyrat (in his restaurant La Maison des Bois in the mountains near Annecy). One of the meals on the menu consists in a small, greenish, olive-shaped and -sized, rather strangely looking ‘thing’ with an irregular surface, which does not look very palatable at all. It is prepared by the chef himself in the middle of the restaurant, for all guests at the same time, and it is ‘cooked’ in liquid nitrogen, piece by piece. When the chef gives it to your hand, it’s very cold, it even burns a little, and it emits strange fumes. Once each guest has been given their own piece, the chef then asks everybody to close their eyes and to put the ‘thing’ in their mouths. Let me try to convey the gustatory and olfactory experience which follows: various textures succeed each other, the ‘thing’ being first solid, and then very suddenly liquid and gas at the same time, where both the liquid and the gas ingredients continue to evolve and transform, providing various stages of the overall experience. Each stage brings with itself a different taste and a different scent, ranging from wood, lichen, fir tree, pine cone, forest herbs,… to simply fresh air. The succession of the different stages provides an overall experience of smelling and tasting the forest itself—a gustatory and olfactory walk in a forest. And indeed, that’s what the chef planned to provide as an experience for the guests, reinforcing the experience by telling a story aloud and by asking his guests to imagine, with their eyes always closed, that they are walking through the forest which is just behind the restaurant and where all the ingredients for this ‘meal’ have been collected. The succession of different tastes and odours is so skilfully done and planned that the illusion is very strong indeed and the overall experience simply staggering.
第1节。在开始讨论之前，让我沉迷于一个简短的传记叙述，并讲述我最近在法国著名厨师马克·维拉特（Marc Veyrat）的餐厅（在他位于安纳西附近山区的La Maison des Bois餐厅）的经历。菜单上的一顿饭包括一个小的、绿色的、橄榄形的、大小的、看起来很奇怪的“东西”，表面不规则，看起来一点也不可口。它是由厨师自己在餐厅中间同时为所有客人准备的，并在液氮中一块一块地“煮熟”。当厨师把它送到你的手上时，它非常冷，它甚至会燃烧一点，并且会散发出奇怪的烟雾。一 旦每位客人都得到了自己的作品，厨师就会要求每个人闭上眼睛，把“东西”放进嘴里。让我试着传达一下接下来的味觉和嗅觉体验：各种质地相互继承，“东西”首先是固体，然后突然同时同时是液体和气体，液体和气体成分都在继续演变和转型，提供整体体验的各个阶段。每个阶段都有不同的味道和不同的香味，包括木材、地衣、冷杉树、松果、森林草本植物,...简单地呼吸新鲜空气。不同阶段的连续性提供了嗅觉和品尝森林本身的整体体验——在森林中漫步的味觉和嗅觉。事实上，这就是厨师计划为客人提供的体验，通过大声讲述一个故事并要求他的客人想象，他们总是闭着眼睛，穿过餐厅后面的森林，来加强这种体验，这顿“饭”的所有食材都收集在那里。不同口味和气味的连续性是如此巧妙地完成和计划，以至于幻觉确实非常强烈，整体体验简直令人震惊。
